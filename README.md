Exploratory Data Analysis on School Dataset 
The school dataset was analyzed to better understand student performance and the factors that may 
affect learning outcomes. It included information related to student scores, school facilities, teachers, 
and family background. 
As part of data cleaning, missing values were filled using suitable methods such as median for numerical 
data and mode for categorical data. Duplicate records were also checked and removed where necessary. 
After cleaning, Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) was carried out using charts and summary statistics. 
The performance of students in Math, Language, and Science was examined. It was found that students 
who scored well in one subject often performed well in the others too. 
The analysis also suggested that factors like parent literacy rate and internet access may have a positive 
impact on student learning. Interestingly, urban and rural schools showed nearly similar average 
performance. 
An additional column called Overall Score was created by combining subject marks to get a better 
picture of total student performance. 
Key Insights: 
1. Student performance across subjects is closely connected.  
2. Parent literacy seems to support better results.  
3. Internet availability may improve learning opportunities.  
4. Teacher-student ratio can influence education quality.  
5. Urban and rural schools performed almost equally.  
Overall, this dataset provided meaningful insights into education patterns and the factors that can 
support student success.
